1 Introduction#

Here are a couple of silly excercises to help you practice:

  • reading data into a Pandas DataFrame using .read_csv()

  • selecting rows using [] notation

  • calculating subtotals using .groupby()

  • sorting using .sort_values()

I work through one example and then you are left to do the other two.

2 Imports#

import pandas as pd

3 Example#

At https://github.com/sawula/Survey-of-Python-data-science-stack is the file named PandasJumble0.csv.

  • Read the data into a dataframe.

  • Select and sort so that the name NELLIE appears in a column (in that order)

  • Repeat for TOMA.

SOLUTION#

First, follow the link to the GitHub repo. Click on the file. Click on Raw. Copy the URL. Etc.

jumble0 = pd.read_csv('https://raw.githubusercontent.com/sawula/Survey-of-Python-data-science-stack/gh-pages/PandasJumble0.csv')
jumble0
Catg Valu Lettr
0 k 1 N
1 h 6 O
2 k 3 L
3 h 2 A
4 k 2 E
5 h 4 M
6 k 5 L
7 k 7 I
8 h 8 T
9 k 9 E

I’m guessing that the letters for NELLIE have Catg of k (and that those for TOMA have Catg h). I’m going to select those out.

jumble0_k = jumble0[jumble0.Catg=='k']
jumble0_k
Catg Valu Lettr
0 k 1 N
2 k 3 L
4 k 2 E
6 k 5 L
7 k 7 I
9 k 9 E

Now I’m going to sort these according to the Valu column.

jumble0_k.sort_values('Valu')
Catg Valu Lettr
0 k 1 N
4 k 2 E
2 k 3 L
6 k 5 L
7 k 7 I
9 k 9 E

Winner!

Now let’s quickly find TOMA.

jumble0_h = jumble0[jumble0.Catg=='h']
jumble0_h
Catg Valu Lettr
1 h 6 O
3 h 2 A
5 h 4 M
8 h 8 T 
jumble0_h.sort_values('Valu')
Catg Valu Lettr
3 h 2 A
5 h 4 M
1 h 6 O
8 h 8 T

Oops. These are upside down. We’ll have to sort in descending order.

jumble0_h.sort_values('Valu',ascending=False)
Catg Valu Lettr
8 h 8 T
1 h 6 O
5 h 4 M
3 h 2 A

Winner!

Ok. Now you’re on your own for the next two files.
